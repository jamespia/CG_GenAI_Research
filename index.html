Navigating GenAI - Skills, Insights and Application

Generative Artificial Intelligence (GenAI) is rapidly emerging as a profound catalyst for organisational transformation, moving beyond mere technological enhancement to fundamentally reshape operational paradigms and strategic approaches. This interactive report synthesises recent findings, with an emphasis on Capgemini's research, to explore GenAI's multifaceted impact on change management, from burgeoning market growth to the evolving future of work.

What is Generative AI (GenAI)?

Generative AI refers to artificial intelligence systems capable of creating new content, such as text, images, audio, and video, in response to prompts. Unlike traditional AI that focuses on analysis or prediction, GenAI *generates* novel outputs, making it a powerful tool for innovation, content creation, and problem-solving across various domains.

Global AI Market Growth (USD Billions)

Global spending on AI, encompassing software, hardware, and services, reached an estimated $154 billion in 2023, marking a substantial 26.9% increase from the previous year. Projections indicate an escalation to over $300 billion by 2026, driven significantly by the rapid emergence and adoption of GenAI technologies.

Key Adoption Insights & Strategic Imperative

Adoption is accelerating across all organisational functions, but a critical gap remains between high-level strategic discussions and practical, daily executive application. GenAI is seen as a primary lever for reinvention, influencing boardrooms globally. Explore the key insights below:

4x Increase in GenAI deployment over the last 12 months.

Nearly a quarter (24%) of organisations are now integrating GenAI into some or most of their operations, a significant acceleration from just 6% reported 12 months prior. This highlights the rapid pace of adoption across all functions.

96% Of boardrooms globally are discussing GenAI.

This makes GenAI a top strategic priority, recognising its potential to drive growth, enhance productivity, and foster agility. This widespread discussion signals a fundamental shift in strategic outlook.

<10% Of executives use GenAI tools in their daily tasks.

This significant disconnect between strategic intent and day-to-day practical adoption highlights a critical challenge for change management. Bridging this gap requires leaders to articulate a clear "why" for GenAI.

Early adopters are already realising tangible benefits, with reports indicating improvements in efficiency (78%), faster response times (56%), and enhanced decision-making capabilities (55%). However, bridging the executive-to-employee adoption gap requires leaders to communicate a compelling vision of the organisation with AI deeply embedded in its daily operations.

Top Risks & Challenges

Successfully navigating GenAI adoption requires proactively addressing data, ethical, workforce, and implementation challenges. Click on each risk to learn more.

The Governance Gap

A critical challenge is that enthusiasm is outpacing governance. 61% lack a formal governance framework. 63% of professionals use unauthorised GenAI tools, risking hallucinated code, data leakage, and IP issues. Uncontrolled use exposes firms to data leaks, IP issues, and flawed "hallucinated" outputs, potentially negating the benefits.

Data Security & Privacy

Risk of confidential data exposure, violations of training data subjects' privacy/consent, model corruption, and ethical/regulatory responsibilities. Continuous verification is needed for outputs to detect hallucinations or drifts. 75% view cybersecurity risks as a barrier.

Integration & Skill Gaps

Integrating AI securely into processes is complex. Only 40% of software professionals receive adequate GenAI training, leading to inconsistent quality and vulnerabilities. 70% of organisations are hindered by a limited supply of data and AI talent.

Resistance to Change

Workforce wariness, established habits, and fear of irrelevance can hinder adoption. Gartner predicts 70% of GenAI Proof-of-Concepts (POCs) will be abandoned through 2025 due to these challenges.

Future Vision: Work, Roles, and Strategy

GenAI is reshaping the very fabric of work. This section explores the future of organisational design and introduces Capgemini's pivotal framework for leveraging AI: the shift from a simple "Copilot" to a strategic "Cothinker."

The Future of Work: People & Skills

GenAI's greatest impact is on people. It's not about replacing humans, but augmenting their capabilities. This necessitates a strategic focus on evolving roles, organisational design, and the essential skills required to thrive in a GenAI-augmented workplace.

Shifting Roles & Structures
From Generalist to Specialist

GenAI handles broad tasks, allowing managers to deepen their expertise. Only 18% of leaders expect GenAI to reduce middle management roles, while 51% expect it to shift to specialist roles.

Human-AI Collaboration is the New Norm

The future isn't man vs. machine, but man "with" machine. GenAI acts as a "co-thinker" and "spartner" partner, handling routine work and freeing humans to focus on tasks that demand creativity, critical thinking, strategic insight, and emotional intelligence. GenAI is expected to facilitate approximately one-third of entry-level tasks, which leaders believe will accelerate career progression. Organisational structures will become flatter, more agile, and inherently collaborative to adapt to this dynamic environment.

From Copilot to Cothinker: A Strategic Shift

Capgemini's framework highlights two modes of AI interaction. Moving from Copilot to Cothinker is key to unlocking strategic value.

‚úàÔ∏è Copilot Mode

AI performs tasks with minimal human intervention. It's about speed, automation, and execution.

Typical Use Cases:
  • Drafting emails & meeting minutes
  • Correcting grammar & summarising text
  • Completing or suggesting code snippets
  • Visualising data from a spreadsheet

üß† Cothinker Mode

Humans and AI collaborate on complex problems, focusing on reflection, critical judgment, and co-creation. This mode nurtures deeper insights and strategic outcomes.

Typical Use Cases:
  • Assessing strategic choices & trade-offs in new market entry
  • Framing complex problems like talent retention from multiple perspectives
  • Challenging assumptions in a large-scale transformation plan
  • Cultivating responsible leadership skills through ethical dilemma simulations
  • Co-creating a multi-year product roadmap
  • Evaluating potential risks and mitigation strategies for a global supply chain disruption
Example Prompts for Cothinker Mode:
Prompt 1: Analysing Resistance to Digital Transformation

"Given our recent internal survey data showing resistance to the upcoming digital transformation among the sales team, analyse potential root causes of this resistance (e.g., lack of understanding, perceived job threat, insufficient training). Based on this, propose three distinct change intervention strategies tailored specifically for this segment, including key messages and anticipated challenges for each."

Prompt 2: Designing Leadership Development for AI Tools

"Our new AI tools require significant upskilling for middle management. Design a comprehensive leadership development programme that leverages GenAI for personalised coaching simulations. Outline the curriculum, key learning objectives, and methods for assessing the effectiveness of AI-driven leadership training in fostering adaptive behaviours."

Prompt 3: Crafting a 'Future of Work' Communication Strategy

"We need to launch a new company-wide 'Future of Work' initiative. Draft a compelling change narrative for our internal communications that addresses employee anxieties about AI, highlights opportunities for skill growth, and promotes a 'Cothinker' mindset. Provide three key communication channels and a rationale for each, along with potential FAQs and their ideal AI-generated responses."
